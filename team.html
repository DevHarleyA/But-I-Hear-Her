Meet Our Team

Swati

Dr. Swati Kshama Rani identifies as mother scholar. She is a multilingual brown woman of color scholar activist and a Lecturer in the College of Arts and Sciences Writing Program at Boston University. During the pandemic she had her daughter at home for fully remote kindergarten during which they co-constructed a play based school called the School of Om & Prem.

Asha

Asha Kshama Mehta identifies as brown girl born in Boston with Indian roots. Asha means radical hope. She attends a small neighborhood school and lives in two homes, with co-parents. She loves to make music and “masti” and co-constructed her kindergarten year in the pandemic.

Betsy

Betsy Salerno holds a Master’s Degree in Interdisciplinary Studies with a specialization in Women’s and Cultural Studies from Lesley University. Betsy’s work has examined female divinity in Indigenous cultures (analyzing how these become dynamic symbols of feminine source and creativity that empower and activate women), the feminine face of the divine, and the relationships between religion, spirituality, ecology and sustainability. She teaches Women’s & Gender Studies at Merrimack College and the University of New Hampshire.

Hannah

Hannah Trivilino enjoys creating opportunities for people to collaborate creativity across knowledge areas, artistic practices, cultural backgrounds, and additional axes of difference. She has professional, performance, and teaching backgrounds in experiential education, feminist theory, music, organizational development, visual art, intentional community facilitation, comedy, policy work, dance, and community engagement institutionalization. Hannah has also worked on political campaigns, performed with world-renowned musicians, and won awards in community building, playwriting, and social engagement.

Christa

Christa Oliver-Torres is an educator, activist, dancer, choreographer, and Assistant Professor of Practice in the Department of Theatre and Dance at Texas State University. Her research and performance work examines bearing witness through dance and using dance as a tool for healing in places where trauma has occurred. Christa has danced professionally in Amsterdam, Austria, England, France, Germany, Italy, Kosovo, & Mexico. She was the dance captain and soloist with the national tour of The Color Purple and was a dancer in the films Avatar and Crazy on the Outside.

Katerina

Katerina (Kat) Triantafyllou (she, her, hers) is a tenth grader at the Cambridge Rindge and Latin School and aspires to become an astrobiologist. In her free time, she loves playing and inventing new games with her mom, learning new languages, writing stories or book and film reviews and exploring new media. She also dedicates a good deal of time in volunteering as an English tutor or as a junior mentor for Science Club for Girls and playing the piano and the violin. She is committed to innovation for social justice and is a firm believer in access to education for all.

Vassiliki

Vasiliki is an interdisciplinarian “surrealist” mom who is fascinated by language in all its forms and aspects which she explores with her 15-year old daughter Katerina in a lifelong work-in-progress. With 15+years of experience in writing and teaching in three languages and cultures in higher education (Greek, French and English), as well as in publishing, editing, literary translation, mentoring and academic administration, she is an advocate of the power of humanities and a firm believer in the interplay of arts, sciences, media, and technology. As the founder and creative director of Citizen TALES Commons, she is looking for new challenges in academia and society to foster creativity, collaboration, pedagogical innovation and civic engagement, and ultimately, have a transformational impact on people’s lives worldwide.
